## What it makes

A faithful recreation of the "I Am Him" deadpan meme music video — chiaroscuro office shots, stark plain-wall silhouettes, big **RED Didone caption pop-ups**, hard jump cuts, a hype-sidekick two-shot, and a bright hero end-card. **Vertical 9:16, ~30 seconds**, for *any* person and *any* brand.

You bring a photo and an idea. Claude does the rest:

- **Rewrites your script** into the format — same shape, same length, new character — and locks it.
- **Builds your character** from your photo.
- **Generates the frames** with the red captions **baked in, in font**, on the route you choose.
- **Renders the clips.**

You never copy or paste a prompt.

## Requirements

- **Claude** with Skills enabled — Pro / Max / Team / Enterprise, the desktop app, or Claude Code.

> **Heads up — Higgsfield credits are real money.** A run is mostly cheap stills (~7 credits each) plus the expensive line: 15-second clips (~135 credits each). Claude confirms before spending on video, and asks **storyboards (cheap)** vs **16 frames (more)** *before* it generates.

**Or the packaged file** — upload `i-am-him-video.skill` directly in **Settings → Capabilities → Skills**.

## Use it

1. Turn on the **Higgsfield** connector.
2. Start a chat — *"Make me an 'I Am Him' video"* — and **attach a clear photo of your face**.
3. Answer a few quick questions. Claude **writes and locks your script first**.
4. **Pick a build route** (Claude recommends one), then approve your character sheet and the boards/frames.
5. Claude renders the clips — captions already baked in. Drop them over a music bed and you're done.

## What's inside

| | |
|---|---|
| **The I Am Him 16** | The canonical beat sheet — steepled-hands desk shots with the map, plain-wall silhouettes, the face close-up, the cash/detail beat, the hype two-shot, the hero end-card. |
| **Script-first** | Your verbiage is rewritten to the format and **locked before anything renders**. |
| **Captions in font** | The red Didone pop-ups are generated **baked into the frames** (optionally masked *behind* you), straight from your locked script. |
| **Two routes, your call** | Cheap two-storyboard render, or the full 16-frame route for total control — chosen up front, so it never quietly burns 16 generations. |
| **Identity locked** | A generated character sheet **+** your face on every frame, because one photo drifts. |
| **Bundled references** | `look-board.png` (the 16-beat target), two example storyboards, and the `scene_frames/` folder. |
| **Gotchas as rules** | Keep the office from washing to black, keep an animated subject from freezing, keep a talking sidekick's mouth moving, wait for stills to finish before video, decline auto-presets. |
